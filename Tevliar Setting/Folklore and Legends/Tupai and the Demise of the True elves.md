There once were a proud people, tall in nature as their very essence was an embodiment of reaching beyond mortal limits to the gods above. They did not worship any but themselves. They did not know perfection for they could always reach higher. 

Many of them split into their own factions, reaching towards like minded goals with their peers. However there was one goal shared by every elf regardless of individuality. 

True Imortallity.

They yearned to live forever, free of sickness, of rot and pestulance. They desired to maintain the pinicle of health, so that their pursuit of perfection could be realized. With age the fear of death grew stronger and stronger. The elves already lived for longer then any other civilized race, yet they did not have the age of dragons. They harbored jealousy of demons and angels alike, being able to be reborn as they were, never succumbing to the rot that waited for the elves in death.

Many elves became liches, seeing all other life as beneath them they ahd no qualms with the neccesary sacrifices of others. Many others chose to shape their souls into artificial bodies, this however was seen as mimicry of the goddess of knowledge and thus was seen as a temporaruy meassure much like lichdom.

One elf, that had risen to the pinacle of all others, is said to have even challenged the primordial god of death. His failure weakened the god enough for Turial to finish the job, shifting the balance of the gods against the elves as Soluna did not take kindly to her sibling being killed.

On a night when the celestial bodies aligned a particular faction of elves is said to have tried to create a connection, a portal of sorts, to the realm beyond gods. The edge. This was the night that Tupai was born, and taken in by the elves.

Tupai was a satyr, not uncommon to find as pests in the regions of elves. However, Tupai possessed female biology unlike any satyr ever born before. She was raised as a child of this faction of elves whom were eccentric even amongst their peers.

She faced much discrimination as an abomination, an abborhance somehow evene further beneath the elves then the rest of things that live. This didn't bother her however as she was in agreement. To her, the only perfection is found not in life but in death. The only thing one can be perfect at was death. She found a beauty in the rot and decay, the end that everything in existance shares. No god is free from death, no imortal isn't surpased by a jealous underling.

A view the elves detested as a thought so disgusting that other factions would do everything in their power to kill her. Tupai, despite her desire to do so being stronger then all else, could not die. Wounds would heal at speeds unmatched. A removed organ would continue to live even when separated. She could articulate her limbs from a distance and they would nearly immediately reattach on contact.

This disgusted Tupai as much as the idea of death disgusted the elves. Unlike the elves, whom desires were self centered, Tupai found immmense satisfaction in granting others the perfection of death. She found so many beautiful ways to lay those around her to rest. The color of blood satified her, and the knowledge that she was able to spread this kindness was enough to supress her disgust at her own perfect imortality.

The only ones whom were not given this kindness were those who fled death. The undead especially, as those who would reject the perfection of death were discusting scum in Tupai's eyes.

The elves did everything in their power to seal Tupai away, and they were successful. For thousands of years Tupai remained in pieces stored across the continent. Generations of elves dedicated to studying her body and what made it unkillable. None could figure it out.

This was until something freed her, and gave her power. Tupai lashed out at the elves. Her hatred at being starved of death, and her contempt at their rejection of it's beauty compelling her into a rage that lasted weeks. She tore across the land, seeking out and destroying every elf she came across. Only one faction survived, the one that managed to seal her away in a pocket dimension of their own creation. The faction that created her in the first place. 
	
Her rampage coexisted with the great slaughter. This is what earned her the title of one of the 6 slaughterers. The rest of the slaughterers sealed the end of the final faction of elves, though rumor has it that a handful of true elves still exist. Though no more then a drunk dwarf can count on their fingers.

Where Tupai is sealed is unknown, as is what set her free in the first place. What is known is that if she were to ever be set free again, life would likely not survive a second rampage. Everything would lay still and rot in the perfection she desires.
